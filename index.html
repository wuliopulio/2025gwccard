Who is Tu Youyou?


Tu Youyou is a Chinese pharmaceutical scientist and the first woman from China to win a Nobel Prize.

Her groundbreaking discovery of artemisinin, a treatment for malaria, has saved millions of lives worldwide.

Her Discovery

Born in 1930 in Ningbo, China, Tu Youyou studied pharmacology at Peking University. She specialized in traditional Chinese medicine.

During the Vietnam War, Tu Youyou was tasked with finding a cure for malaria. She turned to ancient Chinese medical texts and discovered that sweet wormwood extracts could effectively treat the disease. This led to the development of artemisinin, now a standard malaria treatment.

Challenges


Working during the Cultural Revolution, Tu Youyou had little support, limited resources, and no advanced degree.

She even tested the treatment on herself to prove its safety.
